Paddy Diseases

Disease: Rice Blast

Causal Agent: Pyricularia oryzae (Sexual stage: Magnaporthe grisea)

Affecting Stages: All crop stages from seedling to late tillering and ear heading stage

Rice Blast is one of the most destructive paddy diseases, affecting all parts of rice plants, mainly leaves, neck, and nodes. It can cause grain loss of up to 70-80%.

Rice Blast Symptoms:

  • Leaf Blast: Spindle-shaped spots with grey center and brown margin, later causing a 'Blasted' or 'Burnt' appearance.
  • Neck Blast: Greyish brown lesions on the neck, panicle breaks and falls off.
  • Node Blast: Affected nodes show black lesions which later break up.

Favourable Conditions:

Areas with prolonged or frequent rainfall, lower soil moisture, cooler temperatures, and high relative humidity of about 93-99% are more susceptible to this disease.

Disease: Bacterial Leaf Blight

Causal Agent: Xanthomonas oryzae

Affecting Stages: Tillering stage to heading stage

Bacterial Leaf Blight Symptoms:

  • Water-soaked spots appear on leaves, gradually coalescing to form blotches and white streaks from the tip of the leaf to the base.
  • Wilting and yellowing of leaves, often known as 'Seedling wilt' or 'Kresek'.

Favourable Conditions:

Occurs mostly in irrigated and rainfed lowlands. A temperature of 25–34°C, relative humidity above 70%, high nitrogen fertilization, strong winds, and continuous rainfall are favorable conditions for disease infection.

Chemical Management for Bacterial Leaf Blight:

  • Blue Copper - Copper Oxychloride 50% WP
  • Cristocycline - Streptomycin sulphate 90% + Tetracycline hydrochloride 10% SP
  • Geolife Geomycin - Consortium of Plant Extracts
  • Conika - Kasugamycin 5% + Copper Oxychloride 45% WP

Disease: Sheath Blight Of Rice

Causal Agent: Rhizoctonia solani

Symptoms:

  • Initially, greenish-grey oval or elliptical lesions appear on the leaf sheath near the water level.
  • Later, it forms irregular lesions with a greyish-white centre and brown margin.

Favourable Conditions:

  • The disease spreads more frequently during rainy seasons.
  • High temperature, high relative humidity, high nitrogen fertilization, and closer planting are favourable conditions for sheath blight infection.

Disease: False Smut

Causal Agent: Ustilaginoidea virens

Affecting Stages: Flowering stage to maturity

False Smut Symptoms:

  • Spikelets have orange or greenish-black velvety smut balls, leading to chaffy grains.

Favourable Conditions:

Temperatures of 25–35°C, relative humidity above 90%, high nitrogen fertilization, heavy rainfall, and winds are favorable conditions for false smut infection.

Chemical Management for False Smut:

  • Amistar Top Fungicide - Azoxystrobin 18.2% + Difenoconazole 11.4% SC
  • Roko Fungicide - Thiophanate Methyl 70% WP
  • Custodia Fungicide - Azoxystrobin 11% + Tebuconazole 18.3% SC
  • Bcontrol Fungicide - Validamycin 3% L

Black Gram Diseases

Disease: Powdery Mildew

Causal Organism: Erysiphe polygoni

Symptoms:

  • White powdery spots appear on the leaves.
  • Severe infection occurs during flowering and pod development stages.
  • The plant assumes a greyish-white appearance; leaves turn yellow and finally shed.

Management:

  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris.
  • Spray Carbendazim 500g or Wettable sulphur 2kg at the initiation of the disease and repeat 15 days later.
  • Spray Dinocap 0.05%.

Disease: Anthracnose

Causal Organism: Colletotrichum lindemuthianum

Symptoms:

  • Dark brown to black sunken lesions on the hypocotyl area cause seedling death.
  • Small angular brown lesions on leaves, later becoming greyish-white with dark brown or reddish margins.
  • Water-soaked lesions on pods, becoming brown with a dark center and bright red margin.

Management:

  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris.
  • Treat seeds with Carbendazim at 2g/kg.
  • Spray Chlorothalonil 1kg or Mancozeb 2kg/ha after the appearance of the disease and repeat after 15 days.

Disease: Leaf Spot

Causal Organism: Cercospora canescens

Symptoms:

  • Small, circular spots on leaves with grey centers and brown margins.
  • Defoliation occurs in severe cases.
  • Powdery growth of the fungus may be seen in the center of the spots.

Management:

  • Remove and burn infected plant debris.
  • Spray Chlorothalonil 1kg/ha or Mancozeb at 2kg/ha.

Disease: Rust

Causal Organism: Uromyces phaseoli var typica

Symptoms:

  • Rust pustules mainly on leaves, rarely on petioles, stems, and pods.
  • Small, round, reddish-brown uredosori develop on the lower surface of leaves.
  • Teliosori appear later in the season, linear and dark brown, causing leaf drying and shedding.

Management:

  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris.
  • Spray Mancozeb 2kg or Propiconazole 1L/ha immediately after the onset of the disease and repeat after 15 days.

Disease: Dry Root Rot

Causal Organism: Rhizoctonia bataticola

Symptoms:

  • Yellowing and drooping of leaves in the initial stage.
  • Falling of leaves and plant death within a week.
  • Dark brown lesions on the stem, shredding of bark, and the plant can be easily pulled out, leaving dried, rotten roots with black minute sclerotia.

Management:

  • Treat seeds with Carbendazim + Thiram at 2g/kg.
  • Pellet seeds with Trichoderma viride at 4g/kg or Pseudomonas fluorescens at 10g/kg of seed.
  • Apply farmyard manure or green leaf manure (Gliricidia maculata) at 10t/ha or neem cake at 150kg/ha.

Sweet Corn Diseases

Disease: Holcus Spot

Causal Agent: Pseudomonas syringae pv. syringae

Symptoms:

  • Round, small, white to light tan spots on leaves with brown borders.
  • Spots on husks can affect marketability.

Favorable Conditions:

  • Often appears after a rain storm when warm (75-86°F is favorable).
  • Splashing water disperses the pathogen and wounds enable entry into the leaf.

Control:

  • Crop rotation and tillage to incorporate debris.

Disease: Stewart’s Wilt (Bacterial Wilt) & Leaf Blight

Causal Agent: Erwinia stewartii

Symptoms:

  • Linear pale green to yellow streaks with irregular or wavy margins on leaves.
  • Premature bleached and dead tassels.
  • Internal stem discoloration.
  • Reduced yield due to reduced photosynthetic areas.

Favorable Conditions:

  • High temperature, high levels of ammonium N & P, low levels of Ca and K increase susceptibility.
  • Mild winters favor survival of flea beetles.

Control:

  • Use resistant or tolerant hybrids and varieties.
  • Early applications of insecticide to control vector.

Disease: Northern Corn Leaf Blight

Causal Agent: Exserohilum turcicum (syn. Setosphaeria turcica)

Symptoms:

  • Long elliptical (cigar shaped) grayish-green or tan lesions 2.5-15 cm long.
  • Severe leaf infection causes coalescing of lesions and gray appearance like frost or drought injury.
  • Heavy loss in ear fill if infection occurs early.

Favorable Conditions:

  • Moderate temperature (18-27°C) and heavy dews.

Control:

  • Use resistant hybrids.
  • Consider spraying with fungicides and eradicating weed hosts and plant debris.

Disease: Common Rust

Causal Agent: Puccinia sorghi

Symptoms:

  • Oval pustules containing reddish-orange spores on leaves.
  • Premature leaf senescence reduces yields and quality.

Favorable Conditions:

  • Moderate temperature, frequent heavy dews, and high nitrogen.

Control:

  • Choose tolerant varieties and consider fungicide sprays.

Disease: Eyespot

Causal Agent: Aureobasidium zeae (syn. Kabatiella zeae)

Symptoms:

  • Small translucent, round to oval lesions, 1-4mm in diameter with yellowish halos that may fuse to form large necrotic areas.
  • For field corn, rapid drying of foliage reduces grain yield and silage quality.

Favorable Conditions:

  • Cool, humid weather.

Survival and Spread:

  • Overwinters in corn debris; dispersed by wind and splashing rain.

Control:

  • Use less susceptible or tolerant hybrids and varieties.
  • Use rotation or plowing or both.
